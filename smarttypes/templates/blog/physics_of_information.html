The physics of information

The book What Hath God Wrought: The Transformation of America, 1815 - 1848 describes a shift in the physics of information.

This book highlights a story published by a British paper shortly after the telegraph was up and running. The story describes a man killing his wife, and boarding a train before the information of his crime spread. A description of the man was telegraphed ahead of the train, and the man was caught.

The fact that authorities were able to catch the man isn't very amazing to us. But, for people in the mid-1800s, information traveling faster than a train or horse or boat was new. Before the mid-1800s, human digestible information, literally, could not travel faster than a train.

The book highlights a few other stories like this. Andrew Jackson's famous Battle of New Orleans took place after the British surrendered (it takes a long time to send a message by boat).

I love the idea of overcoming old physical limitations.

I also enjoy thinking of information in a physical light.
How does it spread?
What's its fundamental constituents?
And the like..
